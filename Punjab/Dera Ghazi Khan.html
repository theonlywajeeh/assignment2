Dera Ghazi Khan:

Dera Ghazi Khan is a city located in the province of Punjab, Pakistan. It is the headquarters of the Dera Ghazi Khan District. The city is situated on the western bank of the Indus River and is known for its historical and cultural significance. Dera Ghazi Khan has a population of over 600,000 people, as of my knowledge cutoff in September 2021. The city serves as an important administrative, economic, and educational center in the region. It is surrounded by agricultural lands, with farming being a significant economic activity in the area. The city has a rich history dating back centuries. It has witnessed the rule of various empires and dynasties, including the Delhi Sultanate, the Mughals, and the Durrani Empire. The region also has archaeological sites, such as the ruins of Dera Ghazi Khan Fort, which reflect its historical importance. The people of Dera Ghazi Khan are primarily Punjabi-speaking, and Punjabi culture and traditions are prevalent in the area. The city hosts traditional festivals, such as Basant (the spring festival) and Eid-ul-Fitr, which are celebrated with enthusiasm and joy. Dera Ghazi Khan has several notable landmarks and attractions. The Tomb of Sakhi Sarwar is a famous Sufi shrine located nearby, attracting devotees from different parts of the country. The Ghazi Khan Medical College and the Indus Highway are significant infrastructure developments in the city. The region surrounding Dera Ghazi Khan is known for its natural beauty, including the Sulaiman Mountains and the Taunsa Barrage. These areas provide opportunities for outdoor activities and tourism, such as hiking, fishing, and boating. The city is connected to other parts of Pakistan through road and rail networks. The Multan-Dera Ghazi Khan-Muzaffargarh Highway is a major transportation route that links Dera Ghazi Khan to other cities in the region.
